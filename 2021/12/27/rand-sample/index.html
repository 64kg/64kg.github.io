Random sampling

Posted on 2021-12-27 Edited on 2022-02-19 In Science

Basic sampling methods for some interesting and useful distributions.

Standard randomness

The uniform (0, 1) variable provided by almost all languages.

Normal distribution

Refer to Wikipedia.

One easy-to-implement "Box–Muller method": Generate two independent random numbers \(U\) and \(V\) distributed uniformly on (0,1). Then the two random variables \(X\) and \(Y\) \[ X = \sqrt{-2\ln(U)}\sin(2\pi V), Y = \sqrt{-2\ln(V)}\sin(2\pi U) \] will be both normally distributed and independent.

Haar distribution on n-sphere

Generate n independent normal variables \(X_1,X_2,\ldots,X_n\), then \((Y_1,Y_2,\ldots,Y_n)\) where \[ Y_i = X_i / \sqrt{X_1^2+X_2^2+\ldots+X_n^2} \] will be uniformly distributed on a n-sphere.

The intuition is that under any rotation, the covariance matrix will always be identity.
